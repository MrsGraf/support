Keep in mind:
  • The main idea summarises what Trump says in a section.
  • The concept, ideal or historical narrative identifies the familiar American idea he draws on.
  • The movement of the argument explains why this section appears at this point and how it prepares, develops or completes his argument.

Hypothetical example — not taken from the excerpt:

“America has always been a nation of pioneers.”
Analytical stepExample
Main idea Trump presents Americans as courageous people who have repeatedly explored new territory and overcome challenges.
American concept, ideal or historical narrative He draws on the frontier myth and the historical narrative of westward expansion, which are associated with opportunity, courage, self-reliance and progress.
Movement of the argument By invoking the frontier myth, Trump connects his political programme to a familiar story of American achievement. Having presented present-day challenges, he uses this historical narrative to suggest that Americans can overcome them in the same way their predecessors supposedly conquered earlier frontiers. The section therefore moves the argument from a description of difficulties towards confidence in national renewal.

As a connected analytical paragraph

By describing America as “a nation of pioneers”, Trump presents Americans as courageous people who have repeatedly explored new territory and overcome challenges. He draws on the frontier myth and the historical narrative of westward expansion, which are traditionally associated with opportunity, self-reliance and progress. By invoking this familiar national narrative, Trump connects his political programme to a story of past American achievement. Having presented present-day challenges, he suggests that the country can overcome them in the same way earlier generations supposedly conquered new frontiers. The passage therefore moves the argument from a diagnosis of current difficulties towards confidence in national renewal.
